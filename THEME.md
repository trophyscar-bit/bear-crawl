# Plush Crawl — World Bible

The fiction we'll pull from when naming new things. Cross-reference with `ROADMAP.md`.

---

## Core premise

**The kid is gone. The bear doesn't know where.**

The house has been empty for a long time. The bear isn't sure how long — toys don't track time well. The dust is deep, the curtains are closed, and the front door doesn't open anymore. The other toys are still here, but most of them have forgotten themselves: they wander through rooms half-vacant, replaying old games they no longer understand, attacking anything that gets near. They forgot her too.

The bear didn't forget. He remembers her face. He remembers the song she hummed at bedtime. He remembers the slice of pizza he was holding when she walked out of the kitchen for the last time. He doesn't know why he remembers and the others don't, and he doesn't know where she went.

He's looking.

He fights through the house room by room because the alternative is letting the forgotten toys tear him apart. Occasionally a defeated toy is lucid for a half-second before it goes still, and offers him a fragment of itself — a memory, a habit, a way of throwing things. He accepts the gift and moves on. The house keeps going. He keeps going. Somewhere, deep enough or high enough, is something that will tell him where she went. He isn't sure he wants to know. He keeps walking anyway.

---

## What this isn't

- Not *"toys come alive at night."* The toys are not alive in any familiar sense — they're animated by residual attention and habit, and they're dimmer for it. The bear is the exception, not the rule.
- Not a possessed-toy horror game. Nothing is malicious. Everything is forgetting.
- Not childhood-trauma metaphor. There's no abuser, no monster under the bed, no jealous toy in the basement. The horror is absence — a quiet, dusty kind.
- Not winnable in the heroic sense. You can find the answer. You probably won't like it.

The closest tonal references: the back half of *Spirited Away*, *Soma*, the empty-house chapter of *Silent Hill 4*, the photo-album sequence in *Inside*. **Quiet ache, not jump-scare.**

## The Bear

The kid's bear. Black plush, cream paws. Not the prettiest toy she owned, not the newest. The one she slept with.

- He throws pizza because he was holding a slice when she walked out, and his small artificial mind has decided that throwing it is how he gets her back. He's wrong.
- He doesn't speak. He doesn't have inner monologue the way a person does — just colour, weight, intention. The fiction comes from environmental detail, not dialogue.
- He can't be hurt by sadness, only by the other toys' confusion. When his stuffing runs out, he stops.

**Alternate bears** (post-graphics-overhaul, story unlocks):
- **The Bunny** — the kid's first toy, found again. Faster, fewer stitches.
- **The Octopus** — a toy from a relative the kid never met. Throws three things at once because no one taught him to focus.
- **The Dragon** — bought at a fair, used twice, never named. Short-range fire breath. Hates being unloved.

## The House (biomes)

The bear moves through the house. The fiction is ambiguous about whether he's going up or down — the geometry has gotten strange in the years she's been gone.

| Floors | Place | What's wrong |
|---|---|---|
| 1–3 | **The Kitchen** | The oven is still on, in some faint way. Pizza dough on the counter never finished rising. |
| 4–6 | **The Hallway** | Photographs on the wall but the faces are not where they should be. Family pictures with one too many people. |
| 7–9 | **The Closet** | Coats too small for anyone living. Some still smell like her. |
| 10–12 | **The Stairs** | They keep going. They shouldn't. |
| 13+ | **Her Room** | The door has been closed for a long time. The bear hasn't seen the inside since the day before. |

Each biome shifts the floor palette, ambient particles (kitchen steam, hallway dust, closet moths, stairwell snow that shouldn't be there, her-room sunbeams that don't have a sun), and the portal's inner glow.

## The other toys (enemies)

Not feral. Not possessed. Just hollow — toys whose original owners stopped looking at them long enough that they began to forget what they were for. They lash out at anything that approaches because attention itself has become painful.

| Name | Mechanic | Floor intro |
|---|---|---|
| **Brown Bear** | Standard chaser (current) | 1 |
| **Bedhead Bunny** | Runner — fast, weaves, fewer stitches | 4 |
| **The Coatrack** | Tank — slow, three hitboxes on a hanger, knocks back | 7 |
| **Sock Monkey** | Ranged — throws **buttons** at you (current ninja star) | 7 |
| **The Knitter** | Summoner — slow, spawns 1–2 **Yarn Knots** every ~5 s | 10 |
| **Wind-up Soldier** | Charger — winds up visibly, then dashes in a line | 10 |
| **Yarn Knot** | Tiny add — 1 HP, slow, only spawned by The Knitter | (spawned) |

"Elite" variants have a slight visible **moth-eaten halo** — they remembered something briefly, and the remembering made them stronger.

## The five who remember (bosses)

Each boss is one of her toys. Not all of them ever got played with the same way. The ones who remember are the ones who were paying attention.

- **Your Reflection** *(current Mirror Bear)* — A second copy of you. Same fur, same scuffs, same missing button-eye. You don't have a twin. He throws pizza with the wrong hand. The bear's tiny mind has produced a doppelgänger out of stress; killing him is the bear admitting that whatever happened, he was the only bear there. He drops a strip of fabric from her dress.
- **The First** — Her very first toy. Threadbare from a decade of being held. Big, slow, charges in straight lines, hums a song you almost recognise. Summons brown bears to him when he gets hurt — old toys he was lying next to in the closet.
- **The Coatrack** — A marionette of three doll bodies dangling from a coat-hanger. You kill the bodies; the hanger is what's actually keeping them moving. It never speaks, but it nods once when you kill the last body.
- **The Ballerina** — From her music box. Floats around the room above your reach, drops falling notes. At half HP the music box winds down, she lands, and she fights you on the ground with a small bent leg.
- **The Frog** *(final boss)* — A small green frog plushie that was attached to her bedside nightlight. Was always on at night. Watched her fall asleep. Watched her wake up. Watched her leave. She didn't take it with her. He never moved an inch in five years; he just watched.

  He doesn't attack hard. He summons quiet manifestations of memories — versions of you from earlier floors, versions of her that are only ever a hem of dress or a held hand at the edge of the room. At low HP he stops fighting and offers the bear a choice: ask, or don't.

## Endings

A roguelike doesn't strictly need an ending, but ours has three, and they matter for the tone.

1. **You Ask.** *(default if you beat the Frog without conditions)* — The bear asks. The Frog tells him. He doesn't move for a long time. The game loops back to the kitchen. He keeps trying. The loop is what playing means.
2. **You Don't Ask.** *(skip the final-boss talk by leaving the room early)* — The bear walks back down the stairs holding the slice he never gave her. He puts it on the counter, beside the dough that never rose. He sits down. The game ends. No music.
3. **You Listen.** *(true ending — requires picking the "Forgetting" boon at some point; the bear has let himself decay enough to be able to bear hearing it)* — The bear hears, accepts, lets go of the slice. The Frog stops watching for the first time in five years and closes its eyes. The other toys all stop moving at once, gentle as falling snow. The bear sits down beside the dough. Sunlight comes into the kitchen. Credits over an empty house.

## Weapons (gifts the toys carry)

You always start with **The Pizza**. Other weapons unlock through permanent meta-progression and appear in the run only at start or in a Drawer.

| Weapon | Feel | Story | Mechanic |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The Pizza** | balanced (current) | The slice he was holding | bounces, 1 dmg |
| **The Yarn Ball** | utility | From the kid's grandmother's basket | rolls, bounces off walls forever until lifetime expires |
| **The Music Box Key** | slow / hard | The key her mother lost | huge, slow, 3 dmg, pierces |
| **The Whistle** | crowd | From a Cracker Jack box | spread of three small notes, half dmg each |
| **The Compass** | strange | A keepsake from a trip | thrown projectile that returns to the bear |
| **The Hand Mirror** | reflect | From her dresser | melee — bats projectiles back |
| **The Pillow Feather** | support | Pulled from her pillow | short range, but the bear regains 1 HP for every 5 enemies hit |

## Boons (the toys' fragments)

When you kill a forgotten toy, sometimes it's lucid for a second and offers you a piece of itself. Boons come from six **named toys** whose memories are still loud enough that fragments persist. Each has a specific story.

| Donor | Story | Boon flavour |
|---|---|---|
| **The Watcher** | Stuffed owl. Stayed on the nightstand. Saw everything. | Defence + i-frames + perception |
| **The Sleeping One** | Teddy whose owner read bedtime stories aloud. | Slow / sleep / heal-between-rooms |
| **The Tooth-Keeper** | Doll with a small fabric pouch sewn to her chest. Held the kid's lost baby teeth. | Sparkle / lucky-crumbs / crit |
| **The First** | The kid's very first toy. Oldest. Gravelly. | Raw damage + size + knockback |
| **The Magician** | A hand-puppet wizard the kid's uncle made for her sixth birthday. | Weird-pizza utility (orbit, AOE, multi-bounce) |
| **The Ballerina** | Inside the kid's music box. Spins when the room is too quiet. | Tempo / cooldown-on-kill / movement |

A boon's card carries the donor's name and a small icon — quick read for which donor you're stacking. Two boons from the same donor sometimes combine into a **Memory Duo** (Phase 1 stretch) — a stronger fused effect that's specific to that donor's story.

Rarity tints stay: cream / cyan / gold. Legendary cards put the donor name above the boon name: *"From The Magician — Pepperoni Burst."*

## Status effects (memories that leak through)

When a fragment of memory touches a forgotten toy too hard, the toy briefly remembers something specific and breaks down.

| Effect | Reskin | Visual |
|---|---|---|
| Burn (DoT) | **Memory of the Stove** | thin steam, slow weeping seams |
| Slow | **Held** | toy remembers being squeezed, moves at half pace |
| Freeze | **A Moment** | a small frozen-frame border around the toy |
| Poison (DoT) | **Forgetting** | fur turns translucent in patches |
| Stun | **Stilled** | the toy stops mid-step, head tilted |
| Confuse | **Wandering** | the toy moves in random directions |
| Mark / weak | **Worn-Thin** | a yellow seam shows on the toy, +50% damage from you |

Names are about *what the toy is remembering*, not what's being done to it. That's the angle.

## The hub ("The Foyer")

After Phase 3 we add a hub scene that replaces the title screen as the launch point. The bear stands in the front foyer of the house. Dust lit by faint window-light. No music. NPCs are arranged around the room.

| NPC | Role |
|---|---|
| **The Tailor** | The Workshop — spend Threads on permanent stat upgrades. A pincushion-and-thimble figurine that doesn't move. |
| **The Soldier** | Weapon unlocks — spend Spools to add weapons to the run pool. A wind-up tin soldier that has finally fully wound down. |
| **The Owl** | Codex / lore — read entries unlocked by Locks of Hair and boss kills. He's the only one who answers. |
| **Saggy** | Challenges — daily seed, weekly seed, Unraveling levels. A sock monkey that lost most of his stuffing years ago. |
| **Mama** | Run start — talk to her to begin a night. A patchwork quilt the kid's grandmother sewed. |
| **The Mouse** | Secret. Appears in the foyer only after the bear reaches floor 13. Lives in a shoebox under the rug. Will not look at you. |

Dialogue is sparse. Each NPC has 1–2 lines per beaten-boss milestone — slowly revealing what each toy remembers about the kid, never quite filling in what happened.

## Heat / Ascension — "Unraveling"

After the player beats The First Toy for the first time, they unlock **Unraveling** — voluntary modifiers that let the bear come apart further in exchange for more meta-currency.

| Curse | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Loose Stitching** | -1 max HP |
| **Memory Burn** | enemy projectiles deal +50% damage |
| **A Slower Heart** | move speed -15% |
| **Fading Eyes** | enemy outlines no longer visible at distance |
| **Heavier Slice** | pizza moves 30% slower |
| **Quieter House** | boss HP bar hidden |
| **Patient Toys** | enemies aware of you at all times (no LOS forgiveness) |
| **An Empty Slice** | start each run holding nothing — pick up your weapon in floor 1 |
| **Doubled Memory** | bosses always spawn an Old Friend ad alongside them |
| **The Long Way** | floors auto-advance after 50 s |

N curses active = N× Threads/Spools at run end.

## Tonal anchors

If in doubt, this is the vibe.

- **Quiet.** Empty rooms. Few enemies, more space. The house is bigger than it should be.
- **Specific.** "A glass of water on the counter, still half-full" beats "a fountain of healing waters." Always go for the household detail.
- **Tender, not maudlin.** The bear loves her. He does not mope. He throws pizza at his problems. The horror is that he doesn't know she's gone.
- **No villain.** Nothing in the house wants to hurt anyone. They just don't remember why they shouldn't.
- **Ambiguous endings.** The game does not confirm what happened to her. Each player decides.
- **No human characters on-screen, ever.** Her presence is only ever in pieces — a dress hem, a strand of hair, a slice of pizza, a half-rotted photograph turned to the wall.

Names should feel like things a kid would have remembered as facts. *Concrete nouns, plain adjectives.* "The Coatrack" beats "The Shadow Marauder." "A Glass of Water" beats "Fountain of Restoration."
